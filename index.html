The Platform

Student Initiatives and Representation

  • Eagerly represent student issues and work with administration to resolve them.
  • Partner with State Representative Mariano to pass legislation to provi
    • de Bright Futures funding during the summer semester.
  • Increasing Bright Futures to the maximum amount for incoming students.
  • Post about Board of Trustees meeting topics on social media prior to meeting and allow students to post their concerns.
  • Actively seek to fill seats in the Legislative and Judicial Branch to provide full representation to the student body.
  • Work to partner with Housing to educate students on campus on how they can get involved and what SGA can do for them.
  • Create and implement Greek RSO & Housing Guidelines.
  • Use the Judicial Branch to handle lower level Student Conduct cases.
  • Encourage SGA Senators to represent students in their respective colleges by assisting them through the advising process.

 

Transparency and Accountability

  • Host monthly topic driven town hall meetings where students can voice their concerns in an open forum setting.
  • Publish Board of Trustees easy to read summaries on SGA website and social media.
  • Enforce Platform Tracker to update the Student Body on the positive progress that is being made
  • Hold monthly cabinet member evaluations to ensure accountability within the Executive Branch.
  • Build unity with the Legislative Branch by calling upon them to help resolve issues that the Student Body is facing.
  • Work with the Legislative Branch to continuously improve spending policy so funding is used more efficiently.
  • Work closely and directly with local media to ensure truthful and informative articles.

Transportation and Safety

  • Create an immediate parking alleviation by working with parking services to create and redefine parking decals.
  • Continue to reduce partnership with KnightLynx shuttle service and reallocate funds to UBER discount that will be offered to all UCF students.
  • Develop SGA budgetary plan to improve security by supporting camera installation in parking garages.
  • Green Dot Bystander Intervention Training for all Registered Student Organizations.
  • Finalize the installation of lighting in the off-campus Greek housing area.
  • Work with UCFPD and OCSO to dedicate more police resources to preventing violent crime instead of victimless crime.
  • Emphasize the importance for UCFPD and OCSO to make frequent rounds in high crime off campus housing areas.
  • Implement and educate students on a risk management plan in the event of natural disasters.

Health and Happiness

  • Increase awareness of Mental Health Services on campus by creating initiatives with avenues that reach all students, such as Webcourses.
  • Advocate for an increase in free STD testing.
  • Actively fight to defend students rights to wear reasonable attire such as stringers to the RWC.
  • Guarantee the addition of a squat rack in the RWC.
  • Encourage greater dietary diversity amongst UCF dining.
  • Improve and increase trash and recycling bin locations on campus.
  • Partner with Veteran Academic Resource Center to provide more discounts for UCF Veterans.
  • Advocate for free women's health products

University Pride and Campus Experience

  • UCF Shirt Tradeoff: Bring in another University’s shirt and trade it for a UCF shirt.
  • Use SGA funds and resources to enhance tailgate and game time experience Ex: “UCF Football Concert Series”
  • Provide UCF away game bus transportation for one Football game and one Spring Athletics game.
  • Advocate for beer to be available in the student section of the Football Stadium.
  • Hold Pep Rallies at Memory Mall the day before a football game with UCF Athletics.
  • Advocate for programs to unite UCF campuses. Ex: “Night at the Rosen”.
  • Encourage veterans to apply the skills gained in the military as leaders on campus as members of student organizations.
  • Work to partner with Housing to educate students on campus on how they can get involved and what SGA can do for them.
  • Boost school spirit by encouraging professors to cancel classes and campus activities during Spirit Splash.
